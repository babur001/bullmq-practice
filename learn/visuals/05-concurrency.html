Concurrency & Scaling — watch the two knobs

12 jobs, each takes 1.0s of work. Turn the knobs and hit Run. The thing to notice: in CPU-bound mode, a single worker's extra slots turn amber (blocked) — they hold a job but can't progress, because one event loop can only run one CPU-burning job at a time. That's why concurrency helps I/O but not CPU.

Max jobs in flight = 2 workers × 3 concurrency = 6 — but CPU-bound, so each worker really runs 1 at a time → effective parallelism = 2

Try: I/O, 1×1 → ~12s, fully serial. I/O, 2×3 → ~2s, six at a time. Now switch to CPU, 2×3 → back to ~6s: each worker's 3 slots fill, but two go amber and only one burns at a time. Adding processes (workers) still helps CPU; adding concurrency does not. That's the whole lesson.
